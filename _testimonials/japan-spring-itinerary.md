I met Omer San through a mutual friend before my trip to Tokyo in March 2026. I planned the trip pretty loosely, mostly around the idea of seeing cherry blossoms, without realizing my stay was a bit too early for peak bloom. Still, Omer made the most of it from the start. He took me to Ueno Park, where the trees were just beginning to bud. Even without full blooms, he turned it into a great experience by introducing me to hanami culture of the Japanese people: grabbing food, sitting under the trees with blue picnic mats, and enjoying the atmosphere like locals do.

As the trip went on, I thought that I might miss the cherry blossoms this year. But one evening, Omer took me to the Rikugien Garden, where the night viewing of sakura trees just opened for the season. He knew it was one of the earliest places to bloom, and seeing the large weeping cherry tree lit up at night was something I didn’t expect at all. It ended up being one of the most memorable moments of the trip, a hidden gem I wouldn't have found on my own.

Along the way, Omer San also introduced me to great local restaurants that felt authentic and unplanned in the best way. Looking back, what stood out wasn’t just the places we went, but how naturally he adjusted things as we went along. What could have been a slightly mistimed trip turned into a truly special experience. I can really feel how much he loves living in Japan, and it showed in his deep understanding of the culture and his knowledge of the places he took me to.
