I visited Tokyo too early for peak cherry blossoms. Omer-san introduced me to the Hanami culture of Japan in Ueno park, but the trees were just beginning to bud. So he checked the blooming forecast and suggested the nearby Rikugien garden, the only peaking place in Tokyo.

The huge weeping cherry tree lit up at night was a hidden gem I wouldn't have found on my own. It ended up being one of the most memorable moments of the trip. 
Omer-san adjusted things as we went along. What could have been a slightly mistimed trip ended up exceeding my expectations. I can really feel how much he loves living in Japan, and it showed in his deep understanding of the culture and his knowledge of the places he took me to.
