I had a short layover in Tokyo and wanted a noren curtain, a traditional fabric divider you hang in a doorway, for my new apartment. I looked around Tokyo but couldn’t find useful information in English. Omer told me about Kappabashi Street, an area known for its many specialty shops for kitchenware. He guided me through a number of places until I found the perfect noren.

I’m also a travel sketcher, and when that came up he took me to Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden. Even though it’s a well-known spot, he found a quiet area by the water where I could sit and paint. I didn’t realize it was fall color season, so that was a great bonus.

Omer really paid attention to what I’m actually interested in and built the trip around that. He made my short stay in Japan truly personal.
