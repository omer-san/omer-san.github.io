I had a short layover in Tokyo and got connected with Omer through a friend. I usually don’t go for guides since I like to wander, but this worked out really well. I’d been trying to find a noren curtain, a traditional fabric divider you hang in a doorway, for my new apartment. I looked around Tokyo but couldn’t find much useful information in English. Omer suggested Kappabashi Street, an area known for its many specialty shops for kitchenware. It’s not somewhere I would have found on my own, and it felt like where only locals would've known of. We explored a number of places and I found the perfect noren curtains with traditional Japanese patterns. I also picked up some lacquer bowls and chopsticks along the way, which I hadn’t planned on buying but felt really beautiful and well made. I brought a few home for my family and they really liked them.

I’m also a travel sketcher, and when that came up he took me to Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden. Even though it’s a well-known spot, he found a quiet area by the water where I could sit and paint. I didn’t realize it was peak fall color season, so that was a great surprise. It felt calm and personal, not crowded or rushed, and easily one of the most memorable parts of the trip.

What stood out the most was how much Omer pays attention to what I’m actually interested in and built the trip around that. Instead of giving me a generic tour that takes people to touristy spots, Omer made my stay in Japan truly personal and tailored toward my interests.
